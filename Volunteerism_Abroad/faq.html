FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS


FAQ


Can I volunteer with someone or a group?

Of course you can, like they always say, the more the merrier. Make sure to let us know ahead of time so we can make appropriate arrangements.


How can I find affordable flights?

It very much depends on the country you are visiting, but we do always try to pick countries that are affordable to reach. For example, getting to Morocco can be affordable, but it sometimes takes good searching. You can find round-trip tickets cheaper than flying to Europe, but if that isn’t the case you can always try flying into different cities in Europe and taking budget airlines (I like EasyJet) into Morocco. Also, if you find inexpensive tickets to Spain, you can always start there and simply take the ferry to Morocco.


Are there age requirements to volunteer?

If you are under the age of 18 you must be accompanied by a guardian.


Will I need a visa for the country I’m visiting?

It’s easy to find out! Select your desired country from this helpful link by the U.S. State Department.


Do I need vaccines for the country I’m visiting?

Like the previous question, select your desired country from the state departments website linked here.


What if I don’t want to volunteer anymore after I arrive to my project?

If you change your mind after your arrival, you will be required to participate in at least two weeks worth of project placement and homestay before any refunds will be made available.


What if I don’t speak any of the languages spoken in the country I’m visiting?

No worries! You are only required to be an English speaker. It’s always good to learn some key phrases of the country you’re visiting, but you will be surprised how far you can go without speaking the language of your destination.


Do I bring money with me, or can I access my money abroad?

It’s always good to bring a little emergency cash with you, but we highly recommend just using your ATM card (make sure it works abroad) to withdraw whatever money you need while in country. The exchange rate is always exact, and the foreign transaction fee (usually something like 5 dollars) is definitely worth not carrying around huge amounts of cash with you.


How do I obtain travel insurance?

It’s easy to shop around for travel insurance online. If you don’t have time to look around, here is a helpful link for finding insurance that works for you.


Am I obligated to stay with my project on the weekends?

Not at all. Go explore!


Is Volunteerism Abroad only for U.S. citizens?

Not at all! We accept volunteers from anywhere!
